Research Interests

My research aims to make battery management more physically informed, data-efficient, and degradation-aware.

Electrochemical Battery Modeling

High-fidelity lithium-ion battery modeling based on electrochemical dynamics and degradation mechanisms.

P2D model SEI growth Li plating

AI-driven Diagnosis

Synthetic data generation and model-based learning for battery state estimation, degradation diagnosis, and fault detection.

Data augmentation Fault detection Digital twin

Degradation-aware Control

Fast-charging and load-profile optimization that accounts for internal electrochemical safety boundaries and long-term degradation.

Fast charging RL Overpotential

Physics-informed Synthetic Battery Data for Diagnostic AI

Development of electrochemical-model-based synthetic cycle data to improve the generalization of battery diagnostic models under unseen operating conditions.

  • Electrochemical model calibration
  • Cycle data synthesis
  • AI-based diagnosis and fault detection

Degradation-aware Fast-charging Optimization

Reinforcement-learning-based charging profile design that considers internal electrochemical behavior and degradation mechanisms beyond terminal voltage constraints.

  • Overpotential envelope tracking
  • Lithium plating-aware charging
  • Deployable BMS-observable policy design

Metaheuristic Parameter Estimation for Battery Digital Twins

Parameter estimation framework for high-fidelity battery models using adaptive metaheuristic optimization and experimental voltage response matching.

  • Model calibration
  • Optimization landscape analysis
  • Digital twin construction

Motion-aware Battery Degradation in Mobile Robots

Analysis of how real robotic and UAV motion profiles induce distinct battery load patterns and degradation behavior.

  • Current sensing module
  • Motion-conditioned load profiles
  • Virtual degradation experiments
